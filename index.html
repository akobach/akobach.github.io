Notable results:

  1. There is a fundamental relationship in the Standard Model of particle physics between the operator's baryon number (B), lepton number (L), and classical mass dimension (d): (B-L)/2 = d (mod 2) [hep-ph/1604.05726].
  2. The phenomenon of neutrino oscillations in quantum field theory does not require neutrino wave packets, and neutrinos are emitted from the source at differnt times, contrary to popular lore [hep-ph/1711.07491].
  3. The long-standing discrepancy between inclusive and exclusive determinations of Vcb, one of the fundamental parameters in the Standard Model, disappears by removing the assumption of heavy quark effective field theory in the exclusive parameterization [hep-ph/1703.08170].
  4. The correct operator basis for the Lagrangian of a single fermion in an external field was debated for decades, but it can be unambiguously determined using advanced methods in abstract algebra [hep-ph/1704.00008].
  5. Constraints of analyticity and unitarity can sufficiently explain the open question why lattice calculations of the semileptonic form factors of B→D and Bs→Ds are similar within a few percent over the entire kinematic range [hep-ph/1910.13024].
